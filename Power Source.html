Global Power Sources and Their Origins

1. Fossil Fuels (Chemical Energy)

  • Coal: Used for electricity generation and industrial processes.
  • Natural Gas: Generates electricity, powers vehicles, and heats buildings.
  • Petroleum (Oil): Primarily used for transportation fuel and heating.

2. Nuclear Energy (Nuclear Energy)

  • Uranium-235: Fission-based nuclear reactors for electricity.
  • Plutonium-239: Fission fuel for reactors and space missions.
  • Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTGs): Used in spacecraft, powered by decay of isotopes like Plutonium-238.

3. Renewable Sources (Natural Energy)

  • Solar Energy:
    • Photovoltaic Cells: Directly convert sunlight into electricity.
    • Solar Thermal Plants: Use mirrors to focus sunlight, generating steam for electricity.
  • Wind Energy: Wind turbines harness kinetic energy from wind to generate electricity.
  • Hydropower: Dams and rivers use gravitational potential energy to generate electricity.
  • Geothermal Energy: Heat from beneath the Earth's surface used for power generation.
  • Biomass: Organic materials such as wood and agricultural waste are burned or converted to biofuels to generate electricity.

4. Emerging Technologies

  • Fusion Energy: Harnesses energy from nuclear fusion reactions (still experimental).
  • Tidal and Wave Energy: Utilizes the kinetic energy from ocean tides and waves to generate electricity.
