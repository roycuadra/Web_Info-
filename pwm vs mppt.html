PWM vs MPPT Solar Charge Controllers

Understanding the differences and maximizing solar efficiency

Key Differences

Solar charge controllers play a critical role in managing energy from solar panels. The two main types are PWM and MPPT. Here’s how they differ:

Feature PWM MPPT
Voltage Management Directly connects to battery; excess voltage is wasted. Tracks and optimizes voltage to maximize power output.
Efficiency ~75% ~95-98%
Best Use Case Small systems with minimal voltage difference. Larger systems with higher voltage panels.
Cost Low Higher

Power Loss Comparison

Here’s an example using two 16V, 15W solar panels connected to a 12V battery:

  • PWM: Drops panel voltage to 12V, resulting in significant power loss. Total output: ~22.56W (75% efficiency).
  • MPPT: Optimizes panel voltage, converting excess into current. Total output: ~29.4W (98% efficiency).
