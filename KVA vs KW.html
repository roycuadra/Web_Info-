Understanding kW vs kVA

The key differences explained in a professional layout.

Introduction

Explore the differences between Kilowatts (kW) and Kilovolt-Amperes (kVA) and why they matter in electrical systems.

Definitions

kW (Kilowatt)

A measure of real power, which is the actual energy consumed to perform work.

kVA (Kilovolt-Ampere)

A measure of apparent power, including real power and reactive power.

Key Differences

Aspect kW (Kilowatt) kVA (Kilovolt-Ampere)
Type of Power Real Power Apparent Power
Measures Actual energy consumed to do work. Total power supplied, including losses.

Example Calculation

An appliance with 2 kW real power and a 0.8 power factor will draw:

kVA = kW / PF = 2 / 0.8 = 2.5 kVA
