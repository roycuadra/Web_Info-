Types of Circuit Breakers

A comprehensive guide to advanced electrical protection systems

MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker)

Current Range: Up to 125A

Uses: Protects low-voltage circuits in homes and offices from overloads and short circuits.

MCCB (Molded Case Circuit Breaker)

Current Range: Up to 2500A

Uses: Industrial and commercial circuits requiring higher power handling and adjustable trip settings.

RCD/RCBO (Residual Current Device/Breaker)

Current Range: Up to 100A

Uses: Provides protection against electric shocks and ground faults; combines MCB functionality.

ACB (Air Circuit Breaker)

Current Range: Up to 6300A

Uses: Protects large industrial facilities and power distribution systems.

VCB (Vacuum Circuit Breaker)

Current Range: Up to 3150A

Uses: Reliable protection for medium-voltage systems, typically in substations.

SF6 Circuit Breaker

Current Range: High Current

Uses: Efficient arc suppression for high-voltage transmission lines.

GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter)

Current Range: Typically up to 20A

Uses: Protects people from electric shocks by shutting off power during ground faults, commonly used in wet or damp areas like bathrooms, kitchens, and outdoor outlets.
